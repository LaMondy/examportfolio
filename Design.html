COLOR

The colour palet most famously associated with Cinema noir is black and white. Throughout the 1940s and 1950s film makers would shoot using a low key, black and white style that is derived from German Expressionist Cinematography 1920.

Post 1950 as the genre developed, film makers began to use colour to create the same mood and aesthetic, this became known as Neo Noir.

A modern example of neo noir is Frank Miller Sin City a graphic film shot in black and white using red to accentuate against the black and white canvas.I have chose to use black, white and grey with pops of red to dramatise and highlight.

TYPOGRAPHY

Cinema Noir typography is shadowy villains, tough detectives and mysterious women this is the stuff of film noir, the typography usually features high-contrast, black-and-white, dramatically shadowed cinematography.

Font is bold, dramatic and often in capital letters. I decided to choose PHOSPATE sold for the headings because it's thick and bold, which I feel represents the theme well. I'ts easy to read and there's almost a comic book feel about the text which also ties into the sin city style that I have used for inspiration.All other text is in the Bangla MN regular style. I chose this because in contrast to the big bold look of Phoshate, it is more refined and has an eligance about it. A little like the contrast between the rough and tumble lead hero, and the sexy sleep and smooth femme fatale characters.

The largely uncredited studio staff artists who created these typographic compositions were true graphic design renaissance men, who not only painted the scenic backgrounds and created portraits of the stars, but in their own anonymous way, gave birth to a new, pictoral typography style forever associated with noir."
