WHAT IS CINEMA NOIR.. OR FILM NOIR AS SOME SAY?

Cinema Noir translated directly from French as ‘Black Film’ is a visual style of filmmaking starting in 1940 and prominently used throughout the 40’s and 50’s. Although Cinema Noir is not a genre itself but a visual style or mood, it paved the way for sub-styles including Neo-Noir.

Neo Noir adapted to new technologies which saw this style steer away from Cinema Nior's black and White aesthetic.

Attributes of Cinema Noir other than it's visual signature are it's high drama, stories of crime set in a world beaten down by cheaters and thieves. A place where even the hero has given up.

Narratives

Narratives were very convoluted, with many twists and turns, with the use of ‘flash-backs’, dramatic music and witty dialogue and sometimes a voice over narrative.

Characters

Femme fatal, a dangerous man-eating woman that manipulates men using her sexualty to to get wants she wants. Lead male who is seen as beaten-down, loner. Often a detective or solider defined by his ability to survive, corrupt characters and villains included cops, gangsters and government agents.
